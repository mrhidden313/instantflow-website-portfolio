03 Platform Capabilities & Limitations

The InstantFlow Enterprise Platform supports the following communication channels and enforces the following technical parameters:

  • Unlimited broadcast text messaging to opted-in contact lists
  • Unlimited high-resolution image broadcasting and media campaigns
  • Structured business messaging with professional account identity
  • Automated response flows and 24/7 chatbot engagement
  • Live agent handoff and multi-agent inbox management

Technical Constraints (inherent to the Meta WhatsApp Business API):

  • Video Content: Video files exceeding 16 seconds in duration cannot be transmitted through the InstantFlow Proprietary System. Clients requiring delivery of longer proof videos, payment receipts, or extended promotional content should maintain a secondary consumer WhatsApp number for such supplementary communication.
  • Voice Notes: Voice note (audio message) transmission is not supported within this enterprise communication framework.
  • Voice Calls: Traditional and VoIP voice calling is not available through the platform. This is a standard architectural boundary of all Meta Business API integrations.

04 Messaging Window & Client SLA

The Meta WhatsApp Business API enforces a 24-Hour Customer Service Window — a regulatory constraint applicable to all businesses operating on the API tier. Clients are required to understand and comply with the following SLA obligations:

  • Any inbound customer message initiates a 24-hour active communication window, during which the Client may respond freely using any message format.
  • Clients must reply to inbound customer messages within 24 hours of receipt to maintain the active messaging window.
  • Failure to respond within the 24-hour window will restrict outbound messaging to that customer until they initiate a new conversation.
  • InstantFlow's automated response system can be configured to manage initial replies and ensure SLA compliance around the clock.
